The Obligatory About Me Section

I grew up in a house where guitars were basically furniture and someone was always singing — usually whether you wanted them to or not. My dad played classic country, my brothers blasted Van Halen and AC/DC, and somewhere between honky-tonk and hair metal, I decided I liked both. Started gigging in bars at 16 with my oldest brother, and I’ve basically been making noise ever since.

These days I sing and play guitar, bass, drums, harmonica, mandolin, and just enough piano to fake looking talented. My influences bounce all over the map: Garth, Alan Jackson, Brooks & Dunn, Clapton, SRV, Knopfler, Prince, Nirvana, Snoop… basically if it grooves or has a great guitar tone, I’m in.

I split my time between multiple projects: Tim & Katie (acoustic duo), Boathouse (full-band rock/country mayhem), and solo shows that land somewhere between storytelling and barroom rock. I like songs with heart, guitars with bite, and crowds that don’t take themselves too seriously — because I sure don’t.
