The Obligatory About Me Section

I grew up in a house where guitars were basically furniture and someone was always singing — usually whether you wanted them to or not. My dad (lynnbeckman.com) played classic country, my brothers blasted Van Halen, AC/DC, and Clapton, and somewhere between honky-tonk and hair metal, I decided I liked both.

These days, I sing and play guitar, bass, drums, mandolin, harmonica, and just enough piano to fake being impressive. My influences are all over the map — Garth Brooks, Alan Jackson, Brooks & Dunn, SRV, Knopfler, Prince, even Snoop, Nirvana, and Foo Fighters. Basically, if it grooves, I’m in.

I split my time between a few projects: Tim & Katie (acoustic duo), Boathouse (the full-band mayhem), and my solo shows that land somewhere between country storytelling and barroom rock. I like songs with heart, guitars with bite, and crowds that don’t take themselves too seriously — because I sure don’t.
